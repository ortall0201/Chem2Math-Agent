# Glossary

- **Cell potential (`E`)**: Electrical potential difference between two electrodes, expressed in volts; for a galvanic cell it is the driving force for spontaneous charge transfer.
- **Standard potential (`E^0`)**: Cell potential defined under standard-state conditions for all species (with conventions on standard activities/pressures).
- **Gibbs free energy (`Delta G`)**: Thermodynamic state function for maximum non-expansion work at constant `T, P`; in electrochemistry, electrical work relation is `Delta G = -nFE`.
- **Faraday constant (`F`)**: Charge per mole of electrons, approximately `96485 C/mol`.
- **Reaction quotient (`Q`)**: Activity-based mass-action ratio for current state composition; dimensionless.
- **Activity (`a_i`)**: Effective thermodynamic concentration entering chemical potential; equals concentration only in ideal limits.
- **Concentration (`c_i`)**: Amount per volume (e.g., `mol/L`); often used as an approximation for activity with caveats.
- **Current density (`j`)**: Current per geometric or active area (`A/m^2`), used in electrode kinetics and transport.
- **Overpotential (`eta`)**: Difference between applied electrode potential and equilibrium potential for a reaction; sign convention must be stated.
- **Exchange current density (`j_0`)**: Kinetic scale factor in Butler-Volmer; net current is zero at equilibrium but forward/reverse partial currents are nonzero.
- **Transfer coefficient (`alpha`)**: Dimensionless kinetic parameter describing barrier sensitivity to overpotential in charge-transfer models.
- **Electrode-electrolyte interface**: Region where electron transfer couples to ionic species; location of double-layer structure and charge-transfer kinetics.
- **Mass transport**: Movement of species due to diffusion, migration, and convection, which can limit observable current.
- **Diffusion**: Species flux driven by concentration gradients; baseline relation from Fick's laws.
- **Migration**: Species motion driven by electric fields.
- **Convection**: Species transport caused by bulk fluid movement.
- **Equilibrium potential (`E_eq`)**: Potential at which net reaction current is zero for the specified composition; often computed from Nernst relation under defined assumptions.
